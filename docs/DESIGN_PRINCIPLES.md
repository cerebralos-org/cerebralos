# Design Principles

CerebraLOS is built on seven design principles inspired by cognitive science and Japanese philosophy. These principles are not marketing—they are the architecture itself.

## 1. Ma (間) — Negative Space
Active Forgetting creates space for new connections. A system that remembers everything is a system that understands nothing.

## 2. Ikiru (生きる) — Living Memory
Every piece of knowledge has a lifecycle: birth, growth, decay, and archival. Nothing is permanent. Nothing is deleted.

## 3. Yume (夢) — Dream Consolidation
The Sleep Job is not a cron task. It is a dream. It synthesizes the conscious (Core) and the unconscious (Peripheral) into insight.

## 4. Funi (不二) — Non-Duality
There is no hard boundary between human and AI memory. CerebraLOS is a shared nervous system.

## 5. En (縁) — Unexpected Connection
The most valuable insights come from unexpected encounters between unrelated memories. CerebraLOS cultivates serendipity.

## 6. Nagare (流れ) — Flow
Knowledge is not owned. It flows. The Brain Federation enables memories to flow between agents, between sessions, between humans.

## 7. Omotenashi (おもてなし) — Anticipatory Care
The system anticipates what you need before you ask. The Morning Insight is a gift, not a search result.
